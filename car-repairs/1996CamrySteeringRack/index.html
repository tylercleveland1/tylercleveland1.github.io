1996 Camry Steering Rack

Published on 1970-01-01 by Admin

New vs. Old

The steering was always a bit vague when I bought my 1996 Camry. This was eventually quantified when I went to get the car aligned and, according the alignment tech, the front wheels wouldn't even stay in alignment pulling the car out of the garage. After a bit of thinking and trying to measure the play on the outer tie-rod ends using caveman methods, I decided that the steering rack needed to be replaced.

From a step-by-step standpoint, replacing a steering rack-and-pinion isn't really a difficult job in general. One must:

  1. Disconnect the outer tie rods from the steering knuckles.
  2. Disconnect the power steering lines (if the rack is powered).
  3. Disconnect the steering shaft/joint which is probably splined onto the top of the steering rack (while being extremely careful not to mess up their clock spring).
  4. Remove the mounting bolts from the rack.
  5. Swap the mounting hardware from the old rack to the new one.
  6. Reverse to install

Bleeding a power steering rack is trivial, and the only post-install requirement is getting an alignment.

The Toyota Camry falls into a large segment of cars that have a front-engine front-wheel drive configuration. This makes the whole area around the front subframe kind of cozy, and just means there's less room to play with when replacing the steering rack.

Getting the mounting bolts loose that connect the steering rack to the front subframe was really difficult. I did this job on some smaller jack stands, but it would be significantly easier with a lift.

I chose the cheap path and did not buy new outer tie-rod ends for some reason. This was a mistake. I ended up having to replace one of the two outer tie-rod ends anyways during the re-install of the new rack, and should've done both.

Getting the rack out once everything was unhooked was basically like pulling a puzzle piece out through a maze of engine mounts and suspension components. It came out, but just barely cleared everything.

Overall, the job was not extremely difficult. I'd rate the technical skill required at a 4/10, but the swearing factor at a 7/10 simply due to the insane angles required to break the steering rack mounts loose.

Takeaways:

  • Always replace both outer tie-rod ends when doing the steering rack. I read this online, but chose to ignore it. I don't do dumb things like that anymore, and have since joined the group of people saying that you should replace both outer tie-rod ends when doing your steering rack. It's worth it for the insurance on your alignment cost alone.
